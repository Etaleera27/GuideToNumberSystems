Types of Number Systems

Binary (Base 2)

  • Uses only: 0, 1
  • Used in Computers

Decimal (Base 10)

  • Uses: 0-9
  • What we use daily

Octal (Base 8)

  • Uses: 0-7

Hexadecimal (Base 16)

  • Uses: 0-9 and A-f (A=10, B=11, ... F=15)

Conversion Methods

1. Division/Multiplication Method (Direct Arithmetic Method):

  • Used to convert decimal to other bases or vice versa.

  • Decimal to Binary/Octal/Hexadecimal:

    • Divide the decimal number by the new base repeatedly.
    • Record the remainders.
    • The number in the new base is the remainders read from bottom to top.

  • Binary/Octal/Hexadecimal to Decimal:

    • Multiply each digit by its positional value (baseⁿ) and sum them up.

2. Substitution / Positional Method (Stepwise Conversion):

  • Used for converting between non-decimal bases directly (like binary to octal or hexadecimal).

  • Binary to Octal/Hexadecimal:

    • Group the binary digits in sets of 3 (for octal) or 4 (for hexadecimal) from right to left.
    • Replace each group with the corresponding octal or hex digit.

  • Octal/Hexadecimal to Binary:

    • Replace each digit with its 3-bit (octal) or 4-bit (hex) binary equivalent.

Basically, one method relies on arithmetic (divide/multiply), and the other relies on positional grouping and substitution.

Key Idea to Remember

  • Base tells you everything
    • Base 2 → use powers of 2
    • Base 8 → use powers of 8
    • Base 16 → use powers of 16
